### Divine Binding: One God. Forever (Mostly)

At **first world spawn**, the player is unaligned but marked. Think of it as latent resonance, not worship.

The moment a player:

* crafts or obtains a **Rune of [God]**
* and right-clicks an **armor stand**

That act becomes **The Binding**.

The armor stand is consumed. In its place rises a **Monument of [God]**, using:

* The player’s skin (configurable fallback if offline)
* God-specific materials, runes, particles, ambience
* A chunk-bound divine anchor

From that moment:

* The god is **locked**
* Favor tracking begins
* Rival gods become active threats
* Changing gods is *technically possible* but should feel like amputation without anesthesia

I’ll come back to apostasy in a minute.

### Worship Loop (Tight, Brutal, Elegant)

You’ve nailed the core loop. I’m just tightening the screws.

**Prayer**

* Soft requirement
* Keeps favor decay at bay
* Scales with time spent praying, not spam
* Can be disrupted by enemies, weather, rival god influence

**Offerings**

* Hard requirement
* Maintains **Blessings**
* Blessings halt after **72 real-world hours** without a valid offering
* Halting blessings does NOT:

  * Remove rival curses
  * Undo past consequences
  * Pause divine hostility

Blessings resume immediately once offerings are restored, but missed time can trigger **latent penalties** depending on god temperament.

This is deliciously unforgiving. Players who disappear for a week come back weaker but not dead. The world remembers.

### Now the Fun Part: Rivalries 🔥

Each god has:

* **Primary Rival** (absolute hatred)
* **Secondary Rival** (philosophical opposition)

Primary rivals cause:

* Automatic Favor bleed if the rival god is pleased
* Active curse events
* Rune interference

Secondary rivals cause:

* Reduced effectiveness
* Unstable effects
* Increased offering costs

Here’s the rivalry lattice.

---

### The Divine Rival Matrix

**AUREX (Creation & Form)**
Primary Rival: **UMBRIEL (Chaos & Freedom)**
Secondary Rival: **CHRONYX (Time & Change)**

Creation despises entropy. Chaos causes crafted items to fracture, mutate, or fail catastrophically. Time erodes permanence, and Aurex never forgives erosion.

---

**NYXARA (Magic & Arcana)**
Primary Rival: **MERIDIAN (Order & Society)**
Secondary Rival: **MORTYSS (Death & Transition)**

Magic hates codification. Order tries to regulate the unregulatable. Death hoards secrets magic wants to exploit.

---

**KHELR (War & Strife)**
Primary Rival: **VIREN (Life & Nature)**
Secondary Rival: **MERIDIAN (Order & Society)**

War thrives on destruction and conquest. Nature demands restraint. Order demands rules of engagement, which Khelr considers cowardice.

---

**VIREN (Life & Nature)**
Primary Rival: **MORTYSS (Death & Transition)**
Secondary Rival: **AUREX (Creation & Form)**

Death is necessary but hated. Creation that scars the land without renewal is heresy.

---

**MORTYSS (Death & Transition)**
Primary Rival: **VIREN (Life & Nature)**
Secondary Rival: **NYXARA (Magic & Arcana)**

Life clings too hard. Magic cheats the ledger. Mortyss keeps receipts.

---

**CHRONYX (Time & Change)**
Primary Rival: **AUREX (Creation & Form)**
Secondary Rival: **MERIDIAN (Order & Society)**

Nothing permanent survives time. Order resists evolution. Chronyx finds both offensive.

---

**MERIDIAN (Order & Society)**
Primary Rival: **UMBRIEL (Chaos & Freedom)**
Secondary Rival: **NYXARA (Magic & Arcana)**

Chaos dissolves systems. Magic ignores laws. Meridian exists to prevent both.

---

**UMBRIEL (Chaos & Freedom)**
Primary Rival: **MERIDIAN (Order & Society)**
Secondary Rival: **AUREX (Creation & Form)**

Order cages possibility. Creation freezes it. Umbriel breaks both with a grin.

### Mechanical Consequences of Rivalry

This is where the mod earns its teeth.

If your **Primary Rival** is pleased:

* Passive debuffs begin
* Random hostile events occur
* Offerings cost more
* Your monument may visually degrade

If your **Secondary Rival** is pleased:

* Blessings weaken
* Cooldowns increase
* Rune instability chance rises

This means gods fight **through players**, not around them.

### Apostasy (Changing Gods)

This should exist.
It should also be a horror story.

Requirements:

* Desecrate your own monument
* Suffer permanent scars (stat loss, world flags, soul fractures)
* Endure a cooldown measured in **weeks**, not hours
* New god starts at negative favor

Servers will have legends about players who tried. Perfect.
